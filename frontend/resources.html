üíª Understanding Deadlocks in OS

A deadlock is a situation in which two or more processes are unable to proceed because each is waiting for resources held by the other.

üîπ What is a Deadlock?

A deadlock occurs when multiple processes hold some resources and wait indefinitely for other resources held by different processes.

üîπ Necessary Conditions for Deadlock

  • Mutual Exclusion: Resources cannot be shared.
  • Hold and Wait: A process holding at least one resource is waiting for more.
  • No Preemption: A resource cannot be forcibly taken from a process.
  • Circular Wait: A set of processes are waiting on each other in a circular fashion.

üîπ Methods to Handle Deadlocks

  • Prevention: Ensure at least one necessary condition does not hold.
  • Avoidance: Use algorithms like the Banker's algorithm to allocate resources safely.
  • Detection: Periodically check for circular wait conditions.
  • Recovery: Terminate processes or preempt resources to resolve deadlocks.
